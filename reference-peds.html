Pediatric Reference

Pediatric Crash Cart

Quick formulas, PALS doses, airway meds, ventilator targets, and congenital heart pearls.

Calculations

  • Estimated weight (kg): 2 × age (years) + 8.
  • Estimated SBP: 70 + 2 × age (years).
  • ET tube size (cuffed): age ÷ 4 + 3.
  • ET tube size (uncuffed): age ÷ 4 + 4.

PALS

  • Atropine: 0.02 mg/kg up to 0.5 mg per dose.
  • Adenosine: 0.1 mg/kg then 0.2 mg/kg.
  • Epinephrine (code dose): 0.01 mg/kg of 0.1 mg/mL IV/IO.
  • Lidocaine: 1 mg/kg.
  • Naloxone: 0.1 mg/kg every 2–3 min until response.

ET Tube Meds (LANE)

Give 2–3 × the IV dose via ET except epinephrine (10 × IV dose). Dilute in 5 mL NS, hold compressions, administer, flush with 5 mL, then deliver 5 breaths.

  • Lidocaine: 2–3 mg/kg.
  • Atropine: 0.04–0.06 mg/kg.
  • Naloxone: 0.2–0.3 mg/kg.
  • Epinephrine: 0.1 mg/kg.

Electricity

  • Defibrillation: 2–4 J/kg.
  • Cardioversion: 0.5–1 J/kg, then 2 J/kg.

Airway & Allergic Reaction

  • Albuterol: Continuous 10 mg/hr.
  • Diphenhydramine: 1–2 mg/kg IV/IM/PO.
  • Epinephrine IM (anaphylaxis): 0.01 mg/kg up to twice q20 min.
  • Epinephrine infusion: 0.05–1 mcg/kg/min.
  • Racemic epi: 5 mg (2.25% solution) or 5 mg of 1 mg/mL epi diluted in 5 mL NS.
  • Dexamethasone: 0.6 mg/kg IV/IM/PO.
  • Methylprednisolone: 1–2 mg/kg IV/PO.

Hypoglycemia

Dextrose formula: % dextrose × mL = 50.

  • Neonate–1 year: D10 × 5 mL/kg.
  • 1–8 years: D25 × 2 mL/kg.
  • >8 years: D50 × 1 mL/kg.
  • Glucagon (SQ): 0.03 mg/kg.

Pediatric RSI

  • Ketamine: 1.5 mg/kg IV.
  • Propofol: 2–3 mg/kg IV.
  • Succinylcholine: 1.5 mg/kg IV.
  • Rocuronium: 0.6–1.2 mg/kg IV.

Ventilator Targets

  • Tidal volume: 6–8 mL/kg.
  • Respiratory rate: 15–20; obstructive 5–8; ARDS 12–20; <2 yrs 20–25; neonates 25–40.
  • PEEP: 3–8; obstructive 0.5; ARDS 2–15.
  • FiO2: Start 100%, wean as tolerated.
  • I:E ratio: 1:2 standard; obstructive 1:4; ARDS 1:2.
  • High-flow nasal cannula: 0.5–2 mL/kg.

Sedation

  • Midazolam: 0.05–0.1 mg/kg IV/IM (max 6 mg); 0.4 mg/kg IN (max 10 mg).
  • Droperidol: 0.05 mg/kg IV/IM.
  • Lorazepam: 0.05–0.1 mg/kg IV/IM/PO.
  • Fentanyl: 1–2 mcg/kg IV.
  • Ketamine: 1–1.5 mg/kg IV or 4 mg/kg IM.

Neonate & Congenital Heart Pearls

  • Alprostadil: 0.05–0.1 mcg/kg/min.
  • Neonatal seizures: Keep this section for your go-to regimen.
  • Single-ventricle physiology: BT/Sano (semi-permanent PDA), Glenn (SVC→PA), Fontan (IVC→PA); expect SpO2 ≈ 75%.
  • Color cues: Blue baby = R→L shunt, early presentation → needs prostaglandin. Gray baby = LV outflow pathology weeks–months after birth → needs prostaglandin. Pink baby = L→R shunt months–years → needs diuresis/BiPAP.
