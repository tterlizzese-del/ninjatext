Hematologic Emergencies

Heme Criticals

Anticoagulation reversal, PCC/FFP/cryoprecipitate dosing, TXA for trauma, and hemophilia factor replacement.

Weight Input

Use this field to calculate mcg/kg dosing when needed (e.g., ddAVP).

Anticoagulation Reversal

  • Warfarin: Vitamin K 10 mg IV + PCC for active bleeding.
  • Heparin: Protamine 1 mg per 100 U IV heparin administered within last 2 h.
  • Dabigatran: Idarucizumab 5 g IV.
  • Apixaban/Rivaroxaban: Andexanet alfa or PCC.
  • Aspirin/antiplatelet: Desmopressin (ddAVP) 0.3–0.4 mcg/kg IV (→ --).

PCC is low volume; FFP larger volume; cryo for fibrinogen replacement.

PCC / FFP / Cryo Dosing

  • PCC (4-factor): INR 2–4 → 25 IU/kg; INR 4–6 → 35 IU/kg; INR >6 → 50 IU/kg (max 5,000 IU).
  • FFP: 10–30 mL/kg IV.
  • Cryoprecipitate: 10 units IV.

TXA

  • Trauma-induced hemorrhage: 1 g over 10 min, then 1 g over 8 h (if within 3 h of injury).

Hemophilia Factor Replacement

  • Hemophilia A: Recombinant factor VIII 50 IU/kg ≈ 100% activity.
  • Hemophilia B: Recombinant factor IX 100 IU/kg ≈ 100% activity.
