Cardiovascular Emergencies

Cardio Crisis Playbook

Hypertensive crisis kits, ACS anticoagulation, rhythm ladders, LVAD pearls, and thromboembolism dosing in one view.

Hypertensive Emergency

  • Esmolol: 500 mcg/kg bolus (→ --) then 50–300 mcg/kg/min infusion (→ --).
  • Labetalol: 10–80 mg IV bolus (or 2–8 mg/min infusion).
  • Nitroglycerin: 5–400 mcg/min infusion.
  • Nicardipine: Start 5 mg/hr, up-titrate by 2.5 mg/hr q5–15 min (max 15 mg/hr).
  • Clevidipine: Start 1–2 mg/hr, double q90 sec until target (max 16 mg/hr).

Acute Coronary Syndrome

  • tPA: 15 mg IV bolus → 50 mg over 30 min → 35 mg over 60 min (total 100 mg).
  • Heparin: 60 U/kg IV bolus (max 5,000 U) then 12 U/kg/hr infusion (max 1,200 U/hr).

Bradycardia & Tachycardia

  • Atropine: 0.5–1 mg IV q3–5 min (max 3 mg).
  • New-onset Afib anticoagulation: Heparin 60 U/kg bolus (max 5,000 U) then 12 U/kg/hr (max 1,200 U/hr).
  • Afib RVR: Amiodarone 150 mg bolus then 1 mg/min ×6 h; Diltiazem 0.25 mg/kg bolus (→ --) then 5–15 mg/hr infusion; Procainamide 17 mg/kg loading (→ --) at 20–30 mg/min; Lidocaine 20–50 mg/min bolus (1–4 mg/min infusion).

LVAD Essentials

  • Normal values: Flow 4–6 L/min, power 4–6 W, RPM device-dependent; MAP goal 65–90 mmHg (doppler).
  • Shock workup: MAP, ECG, echo, EtCO2, CBC, INR, LDH.
  • Electrical therapy: Defibrillation is safe for ventricular rhythms.
  • Volume cues: Dilated RV suggests thrombus; collapsed LV suggests hypovolemia (give 500 mL boluses as needed).
  • Alarms: LOW FLOW → consider suction/hemorrhage (check INR); Red X/HIGH POWER → pump thrombus (LDH >3× ULN); AMS + high MAP → suspect stroke.
  • Shock treatment: Acute RV failure with dobutamine 2.5–20 mcg/kg/min (→ --); other causes start norepinephrine.
  • Notes: LVAD patients are preload dependent and afterload sensitive; inspect driveline for infection; adjust ECG filter to 20 Hz.

DVT & PE

  • DVT Heparin: 80 U/kg bolus (max 10,000 U) then 18 U/kg/hr infusion (max 2,000 U/hr).
  • PE thrombolysis: tPA 100 mg over 2 h; in cardiac arrest 50 mg push over 2 min.
  • PE Heparin: 80 U/kg bolus (max 10,000 U) then 18 U/kg/hr (max 2,000 U/hr).

Valvulopathy Pearls

  • Aortic stenosis: Avoid vasodilators; definitive fix is surgical. No nitroglycerin.
  • Aortic regurgitation: Avoid beta blockers; temporize with dobutamine or nitroprusside.
  • Mitral stenosis: Avoid beta blockers; surgical solution.
  • Mitral regurgitation: Avoid vasodilators; consider dobutamine for inotropy/afterload reduction.
